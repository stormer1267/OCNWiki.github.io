Staff Ranks

Administrator

An Administrator has an orange badge on the forums and is represented by a golden star in-game. They have all permissions in-game and on the forums.


Developer

A Developer is a person that helps with coding of the plugin (PGM) and the website. They have a purple badge on the website and do not have a star in-game.


Senior Moderator

A Senior Moderator is a long time moderator that gets extra permissions on the website. They have forum permissions for editing, locking and deleting topics and have some extra in-game permissions over Moderators. They have a dark red star in-game and a dark red badge on the website.


Map Developer

Map Developers are highly trusted people that are more experienced in map making and XML coding. They have website permissions for some subforums (Example: Submissions), have some extra in-game commands (Example: /setnext) and get their own server. They have access to the maps and rotations repositories. They can add and remove any map from the repository or the rotations. They get a blue badge on the forums but no star in-game.


Event Coordinator

Event Coordinators are the main people standing behind events. They manage the referees and the livestreamers and manage tournaments themselves to make sure they run smoothly. They have a cyan badge on the website but no in-game star.


Translator

Translators are people that manage Overcast Network plugin and website translations on crowdin. You can visit the Overcast Network Translate page for more information on how you can help.


Moderator

Moderators are trusted people that help moderate the servers. They have in-game permissions to punish players and forum permissions to see and deal with reports but they don't have forum moderation permissions. They have a red badge and a red star in-game. Moderators are invited to the team by Senior Moderators and cannot be applied for.


Junior Moderator

Junior Moderators are newly promoted Moderators. They have been trained and can already punish players in-game but are lacking permissions for website reports and some WorldEdit commands in-game. They have a pink badge and a pink star in-game.


Official

Officials are people that help Event Coordinators with managing tournaments. They are experienced referees that help Event Coordinators with more advanced things like planning matches and assigning referees to teams. They get a star in-game on tournament servers and a dark green-grayish badge on the website.


Ref

Refs (Referees) are players that are chosen by Event Coordinators to assist them in watching and running tournament matches. They get the server ready for the teams and watch the matches and check reports during matches if there are any. They get a cyan badge on the forums and no in-game permissions except on tournament servers where they get access to staff chat, commands to run matches and a star. Refs are not allowed to participate in events when reffing but can make a deal with Event Coordinators for an event. If they decide that the person can play then their rank and permissions are temporarily removed.


Livestreamer

Livestreamers are players chosen by Event Coordinators to stream tournament matches. They get permissions to watch matches on tournament servers and a star. They appear on the staff page during tournaments but do not get a badge on the website. Their names do not have any special color.


Shop Ranks

These ranks can be purchased in the Overcast Network shop.


Dux

Dux is an unlimited premium package. You get a purple badge on the website and a purple star in-game.


Centurion

Centurion is a premium package that lasts 180 days. You get a yellow badge on the website a yellow star in-game.


Optio

Optio is a premium package that lasts 60 days. You get a green badge on the website and a green star in-game.

Other Ranks

Supreme Overlord

There are currently two Supreme Overlords which are McSpider and McYukon. They are long time friends of Apple.They have the same permissions as Administrators and have a dark red badge on the website. Theres not that much information other than that.


Resident Mapmaker

The rank is given to map creators based on their map. If Map Developers think their map has a lot of potential and could be developed more, they give the player the rank. The player gets a blue badge on the forums and their own testing server only for the map they recieved the rank for. The rank is usualy given to the players for 14 days but can be changed depending on the agreement with Map Developers. More about this rank can be found on this forum post.


Internet Famous

Internet Famous is a rank given to players that have created or still create Overcast Network content. Most of the time they are YouTubers or livestreamers but there are a few exceptions. The minimum requirements for the rank going only by YouTube are having 30k subscribers. They recieve Dux perks in-game, access to the nick command, a blue star and a blue badge on the website.


Let's Play

This is a rank that players can apply for if they meet the set requirements. It is ment for content creators to be able to create content without being disturbed. They get access to the nick command and a YouTube button on the website next to their name that links to their YouTube channels. The requirements are having at least 3 videos of Overcast Network content posted in the last 30 days, at least one video has to be more than 7 days old and all videos have to have at least 100 views. The list of players with the rank can be found on the channels page. Like with Internet Famous, there are exceptions.
