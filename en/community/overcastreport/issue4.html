Defenders: Age of Ares

Summer is upon us, and so it's time for Tournament 10!

BrettOgden announced the event only a few minutes ago. This will be a multi-gamemode tournament which will be open to 96 teams. Registration opens tomorrow and the group matches will be taking place on weekends throughout July.

For more information, check the info post and the other topics in the event information subforum.

Development News

The changes to death tracking that Cubist discussed in last week's /Report went live this week. A fair amount of development time this week was focused on fixing some of the bugs arising from that change, but in addition we got a few other updates as well...

  • Moderators in observer mode now have the ability to 'listen in' to team chats. This means they will be able to moderate the chat more effectively without having to join a team and take up a slot.
  • Spawn point generation code has been improved. Map creators can now generate safe random spawn points in large areas, and combine that with spreading enemies apart from each other, which will be very useful for FFA gamemodes.
  • Lots of other improvements under the hood.

Rotation Changes

Conquer and Alpha rotations have had some updates this week.

On Conquer:

  • Wildwood Crevice, Piston KOTH and Green Hill Zone have been added.
  • Valley of the Dead, Aerst Grove, Haunted Corridors, Arid Gold Land and Viridun have been removed.

On Alpha:

  • Trench Warfare 2 and Viridun have been added.

Map News

  • Added Magma Lab Corp, a Fly-Rage TDM by Tywnis.
  • Trinus, by mitchiii_ now has a second version called Trinus KotF.
  • Pontifex Makeover Party is a work in progress map to give Pontifex a new look.
  • Trench Warfare 2 now has balanced kits and you're able to access chests now.
  • Also: Arrows have been moved to hotbars on all TDM/KotH maps if the bow does not have infinity on it (unless you have 2 stacks of arrows, that's just insane, like come on).
  • Time limits have been changed for TDM maps. RAGE TDMs are now 5-8 minutes, normal TDMs are 10 minutes, and anything with gear in it is 12+ minutes.

The Last Life Blitz Tournament

The Last Life, a Blitz event, started its double elimination, playoffs portion yesterday. Only half of the original 40 teams qualified, and it was definitely an action-packed day filled with upsets and tense matches - teams put everything on the table.

JaysonFals, after getting placed in the lower bracket, Round 2, had a long streak of wins against strong opponents. They made it to semis of the Lower Bracket, where they got knocked out by PureBlox, a team predicted by most to get eliminated in Round 3. There were also other intense win streaks. LunGGlove, seeded 16th, won their way into the Winners Finals along with Kyudo.

As in the group stages, matches unfortunately ran past their expected times. Along with the long matches, there were some slight problems caused by teams scrambling to field 8 players. In total, the event ran 3 hours over its expected finishing time, but most teams were very understanding.

Out of Saturday's matches, the remaining three teams are LunGGlove, Kyudo, and PureBlox. These three will be facing off later today, so tune in to oc.tc/live to watch what should be a thrilling close to a great tournament! Good luck to all of those playing!

Staff Meeting

Dozens of staff members attended a meeting last Sunday to discuss a variety of topics from community relations to the new player experience. Though the name “staff meeting” may conjure up images of a board room with discussion as gray as its occupants' suits, the mumble-based meeting was a lively discussion about key topics related to the Overcast Network.

The most prominent topic was the “new player experience”, or how brand new players are introduced to the network. Moderators examined various ways to make the server more welcoming to newcomers, such as making knowledge about the map making system more accessible on the servers, more forcefully directing people to the website and tutorials with incentives like longer temporary donor permissions, introducing new tips, and having casual events aimed at fostering a sense of community.

Other topics included ideas for new premium perks, feedback on in-progress changes to combat stacking, and changes to the in-game private messaging system. As with all staff meetings, these ideas were merely discussed. No specific request or idea will necessarily be implemented simply because moderators liked them.
